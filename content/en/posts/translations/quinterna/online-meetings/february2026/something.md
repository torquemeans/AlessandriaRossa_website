The starting point of Tuesday’s online meeting was an article about the advancements of artificial intelligence, "[Something Big Is Happening](https://shumer.dev/something-big-is-happening)", written by Matt Shumer, a computer scientist, founder and CEO of the startup OthersideAI.

The article, which analyses the ongoing turmoil due to the increasing use of AI-based systems, caused a stir and immediately had a viral spread on the internet. Published on X on February 10th, it was read by 80 million people in just two days.

The author compares the acceleration in the AI industry to the COVID-19 pandemic, at the start of which the population, in spite of all the available information, used to minimise the spread of the virus, until it became exponential, with filled hospitals, an increasing death toll and the resulting lockdown. Shumer showed that if four years ago AI couldn’t do reliable mathematical calculations, two years later it became capable of explaining complex scientific topics and writing software; at the end of 2025 some of the best engineers in the world declared having relegated to AI most of their programming activity. The message is clear: there’s a gap between [reality and perception](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/33/realta_e_percezione_.htm), and this deviation is dangerous because it impedes the population from properly preparing. Society, as a whole, is putting its head under the sand, but this attitude won’t help it face the incessant change.

Jurisprudence, finance, medicine, accounting, consultations, writing, planning, analysis, customer service. All of these jobs will soon be run by machines. If AI is capable of writing code, it can also build a new variant of itself, a smarter one, that will be able to create a more advanced version. OpenAI, for example, has introduced [GPT-5.3-Codex](https://assodigitale.it/gpt-5-3-codex-rivoluziona-lo-sviluppo-software-con-auto-ottimizzazione-avanzata), a coding model that integrates advanced encoding and general reasoning, capable of managing complex activities for a long period of time without losing their context. According to Shumer, the therapy necessary to face the turmoil produced by the development of artificial intelligence, that will happen in less than ten years, consists in individual self-education, that is, dedicating at least one hour a day conversing with those systems.

In reality, what is needed is a new social paradigm, not a culturalist approach. Revolutions are a matter of strength, not of culture. The bourgeoisie spreads news to get publicity and to lead investments towards new technologies, but simultaneously some of them begin to worry about the hold of the system. The ruling class doesn’t have control over the situation but rather chases whatever happens, in step with *“the sorcerer who is no longer able to control the powers of the nether world whom he has called up by his spells.”* (Marx, *the Communist Manifesto*).

Elon Musk announced the [merger](https://www.industriaitaliana.it/elon-musk-spacex-tesla-xai-e-x/) of Space X and x.AI, his company that deals with artificial intelligence systems, giving life to a business of 1,250 billions of dollars. Such an operation is needed to bring AI data centres to space. The so-called Musk "constellation" includes SpaceX, StarLink, Tesla, Neuralink, x.AI, the Gigafactory, X: a conglomeration that goes beyond the American State, collaborates with the current administration, but brings forward interests of international scale. The tecno-capitalists represent economical-political centres of power, such as Jeff Bezos (Amazon), that with his network he brings together cloud computing, delivery of commodities, on demand printing and much more. According to Musk, the future will be a man-machine integration, and humanity will become a multiplanetary race.

In 2027 there should begin the mass production of Optimus, a humanoid robot made by Tesla. China doesn't want to be outdone and, for the Spring Festival Gala transmitted by the CCTV for the lunar new year, has made a show-off of androids. Having for now set aside the ambitious project of colonizing Mars, Musk is going for the Moon, with the idea of sending his automata there to build the first cities. As already stated by the Communist Left, it’s more rational to send robots in orbit, since they don’t need to eat nor rest.

The ongoing acceleration in the technological field should be read through “our” doctrine of modes of production. ChatGPT was released in November 2022 and it quickly improved its performances, becoming fundamental in a series of functions (writing emails, creating codes, etc). According to the futurologist Ray Kurzweild, the [singularity](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/40/verso_la_singolarita_storica.htm) is that moment when the intelligence of machines goes beyond that of humans. When there are machines capable of reading data, recognizing schemes (from factories to radiology, from offices to the judicial sector) such as or better than humans, the [system](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/34/marcati_sintomi_societa_futura.htm) of wage labour gets in a crisis. Our singularity, of the revolutionary kind, is given by the negation of the law of value. An increasing part of humanity is without a job, but it’s not unemployed, that is, waiting to find one, it's simply unemployable since machines went from being prostheses of our body to becoming [replacement tools](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/55/evoluzione-extra-biologica.htm).

There have been set in motion social processes that no one can stop. We give a precise meaning to the word “revolution”: it indicates that thousand-year old historical process that bonds the man of original communism to the communist man of the future. The revolutionary fracture, on the other hand, is that catastrophic process that happens in a given historical moment. The last issue of *Limes* is titled "[L’Italia nella rivoluzione mondiale](https://www.limesonline.com/rivista/l-italia-nella-rivoluzione-mondiale-21175966/)": according to the editors of the geopolitical magazine, we’re in the middle of a revolution, inside which Italy should be able to obtain her little place. The bourgeois can’t look beyond its nose, he measures the grand historical transformations with the metre of national interests.

In various public speeches, Nello Cristianini, professor of artificial intelligence at the University of Bath, explained how in the field of artificial intelligence psychometrics gets used to measure machines’ performances. Humans and machines undergo the same tests and the results are measured. In chess or Go, no human being is capable of beating a machine anymore.

From the automated factories there has evolved a universal machine capable of replacing a good amount of human jobs, in primis those of white collars (Amazon announced 30,000 layoffs since 2025, motivated by automation and artificial intelligence). In a recent [interview](https://www.repubblica.it/tecnologia/2026/02/13/news/ia_microsoft_futuro_lavoro_suleyman_colletti_bianchi-425156449/) released to the *Financial Times, Mustafa Suleyman, CEO of Microsoft AI, has stated: “White collar work, where you're sitting down at a computer, [...] most of those tasks will be fully automated by an AI within the next 12 to 18 months.”*.

The theory of value is clear: a completely autonomised production would be a non-capitalism.

In the closure of the online meeting the winds of war around Iran have been mentioned, a country that has 25 millions of wage labourers, a long history of proletarian struggle and a modern industry. If Iran [collapsed](https://www.ispionline.it/it/pubblicazione/iran-la-resa-dei-conti-228590) or class dynamics were to set off inside it, the impact would provoke consequences in the entirety of the region and beyond. Nobody talks about the uprising that has recently hit the country (that has apparently caused 20 thousand deaths) anymore . In the meantime, angry mobs in [Albania](https://www.ilpost.it/2026/02/10/scontri-manifestanti-polizia-tirana-albania-proteste-antigovernative/) and [Argentina](https://www.fanpage.it/esteri/argentina-scoppia-la-protesta-contro-la-riforma-del-lavoro-di-milei-37-fermati-e-quattro-agenti-feriti/) took the streets, against corruption in the first case and against a law that liberalised the job market in the second. In India 300 million workers and farmers crossed their arms. In the belly of the whale, the USA, protests have become continuous (the next one is planned for March 28th against Donald Trump). The historical singularity is getting closer.
