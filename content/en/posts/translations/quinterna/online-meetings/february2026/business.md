The Tuesday evening online meeting opened with some considerations regarding the establishment of the Board of Peace (BoP).

This [organisation](https://www.unimondo.org/news/il-board-of-peace-la-partita-globale-che-passa-da-gaza), conceived by the current American administration, reflects the changing inter-imperialist equilibria and the need for the USA to address them. It is not a traditional intergovernmental body, but rather an experiment in public and private [governance](https://www.wired.it/article/board-of-peace-debutta-washington-diplomazia-senza-diplomatici/), established within the scope of the Gaza peace plan set out in Resolution 2803 of the United Nations Security Council, which outlines its role in supporting and supervising the transition processes.

Critics accuse the BoP of being a [business committee](https://www.internazionale.it/notizie/2026/02/20/board-of-peace-consiglio-pace) of the 1%. And, in fact, it is, just as States are. The difference lies in the fact that this body is led by Donald Trump, who has assumed the role of president on a permanent basis. Membership is by invitation and requires a contribution of one billion dollars. Currently, the list of member countries is limited (Albania, Saudi Arabia, Argentina, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Bahrain, Belarus, Egypt, the United Arab Emirates, Jordan, Indonesia, Kazakhstan, Kosovo, Morocco, Pakistan, Qatar, Turkey, Hungary, Uzbekistan, and Vietnam) but it could expand: the observer countries are Cyprus, the Czech Republic, Finland, Greece, India, Italy, Mexico, Poland, Romania, and Slovakia.

At the head of the body is not the President of the United States as such, but Trump personally. The tycoon's company, Trump Media & Technology Group, which manages, among other things, the social media platform Truth and is listed on Wall Street, saw the value of its shares [more than double](https://www.ilgiornaleditalia.it/news/esteri/771502/il-board-of-peace-e-la-privatizzazione-della-politica-globale-tra-i-membri-anche-mark-rowan-uno-dei-nomi-piu-citati-nei-file-epstein.html) following the establishment of the Board. The White House has announced that the Executive Council consists of former British prime minister Tony Blair, American secretary of State Marco Rubio, US special envoy Steve Witkoff, US national security advisor Robert Gabriel, the US president's son-in-law Jared Kushner, World Bank president Ajay Banga, and billionaire Marc Rowan. The latter is the CEO of Apollo Global Management, one of the world's largest private equity firms (acquiring non-publicly traded companies).

The BoP also includes other financial entities and entrepreneurs, confirming the intertwining of political figures and the business world. Among the members is also Howard Lutnick, the United States of America secretary of commerce and former CEO of Cantor Fitzgerald, the company (now entrusted to his children) that acts as the custodian of the reserves of Tether, the most widely used stablecoin in the world, whose value is pegged to the dollar. It appears that this exact cryptocurrency could be used as a [payment system](https://www.punto-informatico.it/fintech/criptovalute/board-of-peace-stablecoin-gaza/) within the Gaza Strip, given the absence of banks and credit systems.

It is no secret that the BoP was created to do *business*, to intervene in those areas where new investments can be made and profits can be reaped. Months ago, on the Internet,  images of the hypothetical reconstruction of the Gaza Strip were circulating, whose future skyline is intended to resemble that of Dubai.

Its establishment is part of a broad-ranging USA operation aimed at defending the hegemony of its currency over the world, which is threatened by the formation of new capitalist centres. The blow dealt to Venezuela and the threatened intervention in Iran are part of the attempt to restore the dominance of the dollar, given that both countries were trading commodities and energy in alternative currencies. With the action in Venezuela, even if oil extraction costs are significant due to its depth and density, it is evident that the USA has secured an energy piggy bank for the future. Due to their very structure, the USA cannot do without controlling the flows of value on a global scale.

The BoP's statute was presented and signed at the last WEF summit last January, and specifies that the new institution seeks to *“promote stability, restore dependable and lawful governance, and secure enduring peace in areas affected or threatened by conflict.”*

The USA is proceeding along the path of dismantling the old world order, the scaffold established after World War II, which is no longer responsive to their needs. The outdated international institutions, starting with the UN, appear incapable of [intervening](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cr579mdv4m7o), while billionaire entrepreneurs need to bypass bureaucratic obstacles to pool significant resources that, through their projects, could supposedly revitalise economies, rebuild devastated areas like Gaza, and, at the same time, generate economic returns. Once again, there is hope in brick as a “fundamental” asset to attract capital desperately seeking valorisation.

The national security of a superpower like the USA is now in the hands of private companies such as Palantir Technologies and SpaceX. In 1961, president Dwight D. Eisenhower warned the country about the risk posed by the unification of the military apparatus and the private war industry; today, this dynamic has reached its extreme consequences. In the latest phase of capitalism, it is Capital that dominates over States, even the most powerful, and not the other way around.

Last September, while convening in Quantico (Virginia) with top American generals stationed around the world, Trump stated that the real enemy is the enemy within. In the “belly of the whale,” enormous contradictions are accumulating, ready to explode (“[Teoria e prassi della nuova politiguerra americana](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/11/invasione_iraq.htm)"[^1]). The success of American projects abroad depends on the ability to counter internal disintegrative pressures. The deployment of the National Guard on domestic soil and the round-ups conducted by ICE militias demonstrate what is at stake.

China is currently automating its ports, among other things. During the Chinese New Year celebrations, Běijīng showcased highly skilled martial arts robots. In the US, Tesla's robot, Optimus, which is expected to outpace the competition and is set to be marketed in 2027, is in the works. Beyond the actual usefulness of humanoid automatons, what is underway is a very rapid phase transition. The bourgeoisie copies living organisms and tries to reproduce them mechanically, but what makes the difference is not the spectacles for the masses, but rather the [application](https://www.corriere.it/economia/finanza/26_febbraio_26/giugno-2028-la-grande-crisi-disoccupazione-usa-al-10-2-e-crollo-in-borsa-se-l-ai-mantiene-le-promesse-la-vera-partita-e-sul-lavoro-ae27b3ba-1ee2-4a70-be9f-a9750e1baxlk.shtml) of robotics to industry, which is everywhere [replacing](https://www.wired.it/article/cina-impatto-travolgente-ai-occupazione-risposta-manuale/) human labour.

Even Trump or the chief of staff of the American army could be replaced by an algorithm. BlackRock, the world's largest investment fund, utilises *[Aladdin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DQyyVQcZhqo&t=6s)*, a *software* capable of processing enormous masses of data and determining strategies and lines of intervention from a financial point of view. Financial analysts in the flesh still exist, but no individual is able to sift through and process the sheer volume of information produced by the markets, generating an output comparable to that of these systems.

The development of AI systems is not a fly-wheel for the economy, but rather a mine destined to blow up the system of [wage labour](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/19/automazione_totale.htm). In this general reversal of praxis, it is beginning to dawn on some that this is not simply an enhancement of machines, but an evolutionary mechanism of the machines themselves, a crossing of a threshold. Humans have [externalised](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/55/evoluzione-extra-biologica.htm) many of their capabilities into machines, which have in turn embarked on their own evolutionary history, co-evolving with the society they are a part of.

How far can the replacement of men with machines in productive processes go? How many soldiers would be willing to die for an algorithm? How many workers would accept being commanded by a piece of software? Soon, all jobs that can be performed using a computer will be replaced by AI systems, a process already underway in both industry and warfare. Some technology experts raise alarms and call for a halt on the development of AI, but in the face of [autonomised](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/17/autonomizzazione.htm) capital that answers only to itself, such appeals are of little use.

At the closure of the online conference, the [general strike](https://www.chicago86.org/lotte-in-corso/asia/india/1445-trecento-milioni-di-lavoratori-indiani-hanno-incrociato-le-braccia) in India was reported, which involved around 300 million farmers and workers, as well as the one in Argentina, where a new [labour law](https://www.chicago86.org/lotte-in-corso/americhe/argentina/1443-sciopero-generale-in-argentina-contro-la-legge-di-modernizzazione-del-lavoro) was introduced. In the South American country (as, recently, also in Greece), the historic “conquests” of the labour movement are being swept away, including the 8-hour workday (one may now work up to 12 hours). Proletarians have no guarantees to defend within this society, they only have to free themselves from their chains. Today, everyone has a smartphone in their pocket, now equipped with AI systems, and could begin to make intelligent use of it. The bourgeoisie's research centres observe that the squares are filling up and the ballot boxes are emptying, but they do not admit (at least for now) that the current mode of production has reached the end of the line.
