Tuesday’s online meeting began with analysis of the modalities and consequences of the attack conducted by the USA and Israel against Iran.

As was written in the last report, the war is the USA's attempt to preserve, by any means necessary, the supremacy of its currency. Venezuela, just like Iran, used to export oil and raw materials that, for example, [China](www.reuters.com/business/energy/chinas-heavy-reliance-iranian-oil-imports-2026-01-13/) was paid for in yuan or through bilateral agreements aimed at the construction of infrastructure.

By now the United States is “playing with uncovered cards”, while the ideological appeals to international law have disappeared. With 13 million barrels per day, it is one of the major producers of oil, followed by Saudi Arabia, which produces 10 million; Iran’s production, on the other hand, is about 3.5 million. Therefore, the USA’s goal is not to obtain oil, but rather to exclude everybody else from the control of the flows of value, forcing the rest of the world (China, India, etc.) to use the dollar (“[Teoria e prassi della nuova politiguerra americana](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/11/invasione_iraq.htm)” [^2]). Marx writes: *“All ground-rent is surplus-value, the product of surplus-labour”* (*Capital*, Volume III, chapter XXXVII); thus, when we talk about [oil](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/31/energia_e_materie_prime.htm), we refer to the control of surplus value on a global scale.

As the “Italian” Communist Left said, the [Planet is small](https://www.quinterna.org/archivio/filitempo/059_1950_pianetapiccolo.htm) and there’s not enough space for everyone.

The particular goals of the USA don’t exactly align with those of Israel, which for years has been trying to eliminate the so-called Axis of Resistance led by Tehran. The drawing out of the wars in Gaza, the West Bank and Lebanon means that thousands of Israeli reservists aren’t able to work; industry is out of breath and tourism has practically disappeared.

Iran is a [regional power](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/ennepiuuno/quale_rivoluzione_in_iran/prefazione-1.htm) with a medium range power projection and has its own area of influence in the Middle East, thanks to a network of alliances and the subsidising of local dissidents (Hamas in Gaza, Hezbollah in Lebanon, Assad in Syria until his downfall, and the Houthis in Yemen). The “Twelve-Day War”, started by Israel last June, hasn’t “solved” anything, but it has been a useful test (assessment) of Iran's defensive capabilities and helped locate its weak points. The *regime change* operation, launched by the USA and Israel, does not seem to have been successful and the conflict might extend over time. The increase in oil and gas prices might worsen inflation, during an already critical situation for the global economy. Meanwhile, European countries are trying to not get involved in the war, though moving in a scattered manner and without any coordination.

Regarding the strategic moves on the field, Americans and Israelis had prepared a *wargame* that forecasted the decapitation of the Iranian leadership (beside Khamenei, dozens of generals have been killed) and the destruction of missile plants and military posts; likewise, Iran had elaborated its own plans, aimed at the [regionalisation](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/1/us-israel-attacks-on-iran-death-toll-and-injuries-live-tracker) of the conflict, feasible even in the case where political and military leaders would be eliminated. Sure enough, as a response to the first attacks of Saturday 28 of February, missiles and drones were immediately launched towards Israel and against multiple targets in neighbouring Gulf countries, such as military bases and American embassies, oil refineries, harbours but also civil structures, like the hotel-symbol Burj Khalifa in Dubai.

Iran is putting strategic pressure over [nearby](https://www.internazionale.it/notizie/noura-doukhi/2026/03/02/conseguenze-guerra-iran-paesi-golfo) countries, causing internal political and economic repercussions: Dubai, a luxurious tourist destination, is quickly getting gutted; Qatar has momentarily halted the extraction of gas; the strong Shiite population (beyond 50%) of Bahrain is starting to show troubling signs. Ayatollah Khamenei wasn’t only a key figure for Iran, but he was also a religious symbol that made him only second to Ali al-Sistani, highest figure for Shiites all over the world. Countries like Qatar, Bahrain, United Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia and Jordan have fragile state structures (held by thousands of immigrant proletarians) whose economies are mainly based on rent); the petromonarchies aren’t equipped to withstand a prolonged and extended conflict. Iran, on the other hand, is a modern state with a thousand-years-old united history. A country with over 85 million inhabitants, it is five times bigger than Italy (1.6 millions of square kilometres) and it’s highly improbable that someone might think of occupying it.

The time factor is important: if the Iranians endure for a few weeks they might create serious problems for the supply of ammunition to Israel and the USA. The *Economist*, in the article “[Are Gulf States running out of missile interceptors?](https://www.economist.com/middle-east-and-africa/2026/03/03/are-gulf-states-running-out-of-missile-interceptors)”, highlights how Gulf countries are quickly running out of missile interceptors. In the first Gulf War, in 1991, Saddam Hussein launched 42 missiles against Israel and 46 against Saudi Arabia; during the first three days of the Israeli-American attack, the Iranian army launched more that 300 ballistic missiles towards Israel and the United Arab Emirates. For Iran, sending a drone is less expensive than what it costs for the USA and its regional allies to strike it down. Tehran has also [hit](https://it.euronews.com/next/2026/03/03/palantir-aws-e-discussioni-antropiche-come-viene-utilizzata-lia-contro-liran) a *datacenter* of Amazon Web Services and Microsoft in the United Arab Emirates and in Bahrain. This conflict has caused one of the biggest aerial blockades in history, with more than twelve thousand  flights cancelled.

The lengthening of the war for weeks or even months, with the partial [blockade](https://stream24.ilsole24ore.com/video/mondo/iran-chiuso-stretto-hormuz-schizzano-prezzi-benzina-e-gas/AIKiOgkB) of the Hormuz strait, will cause the slow down of international trade. There might also be a blockade of Bab el-Mandeb strait, considering that the [Houthis](https://www.trasportoeuropa.it/notizie/marittimo/nuova-minaccia-degli-houthi-sulle-rotte-di-suez) are sympathetic to Iran. This regional conflict might thus become global and,to some extent, it already is: the attacks have reached Cyprus, a member state of the European Union, where a British military base has been hit. Very violent clashes have been happening between Pakistan and Afghanistan, with Islamabad bombing Taliban's positions. It’s well known how conflicts start, but it's always hard to forecast their outcome. 

The time factor is looking to be against the USA, and Israel, seeing as how the first cracks are beginning to form inside the MAGA “galaxy”, of which Trump makes himself the spokesman. Trump was elected with the promise of ending all wars, but in reality he’s starting new ones. One of the MAGA representatives against the attack on Iran is the vice president JD Vance, but there are discordant voices inside the apparatus (Pentagon, the army) too. Demonstrations against the war have been organised in New York, Washington and in several other American cities.

The increasing difficulties in selling USA’s treasury bonds are leading to an increase in bond yields. The world is starting to disengage itself from the support of the American debt, preferring to seek refuge elsewhere, for example in gold.

 The ongoing crisis can be understood starting by the funnel diagram (figure 6) displayed in the article “[Un modello dinamico di crisi](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/24/modello_dinamico_01.htm)”(*n*+1, n. 24) [^3], in which we can observe that the GDP synchronization of the various countries naturally approximates to zero. The schema portrays the relative increments of industrial production of the major countries from 1914 to 2008 and this evolution mirrors the one of the rate of profit. The valorization global crisis produces an explosion of public debt in various countries,  particularly the USA, causing the necessity to “intercept” shares of world surplus value, a single lymph for an asphyxiated system. The historic crisis of senile capitalism determines an acceleration of the clash, by this point automatic, between capitalistic powers.

It’s not the blood thirst of capitalists to produce wars, as some *luogocomunisti*[^4] write, but the anonymous and impersonal working mechanisms of capitalism. The same mechanisms that determine the necessity to “liberalize” the job market, as in the case of [Argentina](https://www.chicago86.org/lotte-in-corso/americhe/argentina/1443-sciopero-generale-in-argentina-contro-la-legge-di-modernizzazione-del-lavoro) or Greece, that has brought back by law the possibility to extend the working day up to 12 hours.

Numerous processes are operating  that, for ease of explanation, we treat separately (war, economy, technology, etc.), in reality they’re part of an unique dynamic, the one that leads to the “[historical singularity](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/40/verso_la_singolarita_storica.htm)”.
