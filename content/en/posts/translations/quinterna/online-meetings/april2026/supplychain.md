Last Tuesday evening’s online meeting started with an update on the latest news relatinged to the war in Iran.

After the failure of negotiations between the United States and Iran that occurred in Islamabad, Pakistan, the Trump administration has announced a [blockade](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/12/trump-iran-war-strait-of-hormuz.html) of the Strait of Hormuz. The closure will have immediate [repercussions](https://www.adnkronos.com/internazionale/esteri/iran-blocco-hormuz-come-funziona-gli-scenari_7jaq0NnV6808Z0xohNfj6f) both on the global oil traffic and on the supply of goods and raw material to and from Tehran.

War, as we often state, is not a governable phenomenon: it produces effects that have the power to get away from any regulation. The blockade started on the 13th of April by the USA constitutes—as reported by *The Economist*—a risky gamble, which may aggravate the global energy crisis and trigger a new escalation. (“[Donald Trump's blockade of Hormuz is a dangerous gamble](https://www.economist.com/middle-east-and-africa/2026/04/13/donald-trumps-blockade-of-hormuz-is-a-dangerous-gamble)”)

The recent dispute between the Trump administration and the Catholic Church, which has taken a clear position in the debate over the conflict and over American policy, is significant. Strong in its two-thousand year tradition, and with Pope Francis, the Church has already warned against the danger of a Third World War.

We are watching a crisis that our guiding school of thought has defined as the "[Imperialism of Aircraft Carriers](https://www.quinterna.org/archivio/1952_1970/imperialismo_portaerei.htm)" (1957):

> *"Capitalist imperialism is first of all a hegemony over the world market. However, to conquer such supremacy, it does not suffice to have just a mighty industrial machine and a territory with sufficient raw material. It requires a grand navy, both mercantile and military, which is the means through which the great intercontinental routes are controlled. Historical events, in fact, show how the succession of imperialist supremacy is strictly linked, under the system of capitalist mercantilism, to the succession of naval supremacy."*

The imperialism of aircraft carriers has been characterized by the control of oceans and seas, not in the sense of capillary dominion of every single square kilometer, but rather as garrison of the main strategic points – ports, straits, *[choke points](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oVlXkCdQh9c)* – which constitute obligatory passages: Suez, Panama, Malacca, Bab el-Mandeb, Hormuz, Gibraltar. The sea connects the mainland, where surplus value is produced; not coincidentally, 90% of commodities travel by [boat](https://www.logisticanews.it/trasporto-marittimo-re-della-distribuzione-globale-il-90-dei-volumi-e-suo/). When talking about the USA’s decline we’re not referring here to a gradual process, but to longstanding accumulations that find an irregular resolution, like a beam carrying an ever growing load: first it creaks, then it breaks. It is what the Communist Left defines as a “catastrophic transition”.

The blockade of Hormuz by the USA is part of the *wargame* that involves the entire world: from the Gulf countries to China, reaching the European continent. We’re in the phase of *“warpolitik”*[^1], a term that indicates the merging of politics and war into one, already theorised by Carl von Clausewitz. The role of the United States as a global *rentier*, predicated on its military supremacy, appears today to be in profound crisis. In the article [“Teoria e prassi della politiguerra americana”](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/11/invasione_iraq.htm)[^2] we wrote: 
*"Warpolitik is the long arm of the State. Given the current situation, global capital would need a global State and the strategy of the United States then, to be coherent with itself, should be directed towards this objective. But, since that is impossible to realise, they are bound to move on the plane of old national imperialism. But then they face an insurmountable contradiction, linked to the fact that they cannot apply the political strategies of a past historical period to the present historical dynamics."*

Today there is no sign of a new world order capable of replacing American-lead imperialism and holding together global capitalism. If the USA were to lose control of its strategic nodes, there would be drastic repercussions for the stability of the dollar.

Iran has announced its wish to impose a fee on ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz, with payment available in both cryptocurrency and yuan. The area is controlled through the usage of mobile missile sites along the coasts, small, agile boats and “intelligent” mines positioned on the sea floor. In opposition to the imperialism of aircraft carriers, these military resources are more limited, yet still able to bridge the gap and establish a *symmetry*.

Some weeks ago, *The Economist*, in their article "The Nightmare Scenario for Global Trade", analysed the commercial flows that pass through the various straits, bringing attention to the fact that the expansion and the prolongation of the conflict could beget additional setbacks and new waterway clogs. After the Suez Canal blockage in 2021 (caused by a cargo ship that crashed into the canal wall), critical issues in the Bab el-Mandeb strait have also been recorded following the Houthi offensive. Similarly, during the pandemic of COVID-19, *lockdowns* had provoked halts and severe slowdowns of world maritime traffic. In the case of the Strait of Hormuz, troubles in supplying fertilisers for agriculture have already appeared, and many in the UN Food and Agriculture Organization warn that a prolonged closure could trigger a [global food crisis](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/14/fao-warns-strait-of-hormuz-disruption-risks-triggering-a-global-food-crisis).
In the 1950s the Left wrote that [commodities will never feed mankind](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/storici/maimerce.htm). Production for the realm of commodities does not feed mankind because it does not defer to the needs of humans, but to the valorisation of capital. In the world market, cereal grains are produced in some regions, in others petroleum is extracted, and from others, again, different raw materials are exported. If supply chain logistics were to be interrupted in a clear and continuous manner, whole populations would rapidly find themselves without food, fuel, and energy. It is, in final analysis, a problem of an existential nature for the species.

The prime capitalist nations report rates of growth near zero, the encephalogram of capitalism has shown a flat line for years at this point, and this last armed crisis will only [exacerbate](https://www.reuters.com/business/imf-cuts-growth-outlook-warns-potential-global-recession-if-iran-war-worsens-2026-04-14/) the already grave economic situation. In this context, the bourgeoisie’s ideological capitulations towards marxism have turned increasingly more numerous; the geopolitical magazine *Limes*, in its third 2026 issue, asserts that there is presently a *"permanent revolution to preserve what exists"* (“Bussando alla porta dell’inferno”[^3]).
*Limes* also focuses on the role of [Türkiye](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2vjyhE72M0o), which in the dispute for the mediterranean region arises as an essential actor, in line with much of what we have written in the article [L'Europa virtuale e i nuovi attrattori d'Eurasia: la Turchia come fulcro dinamico](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/23/turchia1.htm)[^4]. Even in the case where Israel does manage to break the Shiite axis lead by Iran, or at least weaken it, there remains another enemy, represented precisely by Türkiye, that is, by the Sunni axis.

The war initiated with the attack by *Hamas* on October 7th 2023 will not exhaust itself given an accord with *Iran*. Türkiye shapes up to be the bridge between the war in Ukraine and the Mediterranean faultline. Another significant actor is Egypt, which controls the Suez Canal.

Egypt, Türkiye, Saudi Arabia, and Pakistan have recently started a [pact](https://www.middleeasteye.net/news/turkey-saudi-arabia-egypt-and-pakistan-meet-ankara-pushes-security-pact) with the objective of developing cooperation between their defense sectors. Pakistan, a nuclear power, [deployed](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/saudi-arabia-says-pakistan-sends-fighter-jets-kingdom-under-defence-pact-2026-04-11/) soldiers and military vehicles to Saudi Arabia as to follow bilateral agreements. Türkiye, for its part, has for years been giving (military support)[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qatar–Turkey_relations] to Qatar in exchange for supplies of gas and financial investments. These “forms of collaboration” aren’t looked upon kindly by the USA or Israel.

World War 3 will also be fought in pieces, as Pope Francis affirmed, but these pieces have begun to weld. In the article “(Un modello dinamico di crisi)[https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/24/modello_dinamico_01.htm]”[^5] we highlighted the trend of the system and, basing ourself on data and time projections, we pinpointed a possible point of no return by around 2030.

Also in the social point of view the capitalist system is increasingly chaotic and unstable. Many computer software companies and entrepreneurs throw warnings at the recent development of Artificial Intelligence. In the field of finances, there is fear that [Mythos](https://www.ilpost.it/2026/04/11/anthropic-ai-governo-stati-uniti-mythos-falle-sicurezza-banche/), the new cutting-edge AI model by Anthropic, might put the security of banks, credit institutions and states  in danger, since it's capable of finding vulnerabilities within computer systems today not yet discovered by human programmers and to the various technologies of supervision. It’s likewise true that such a model might be used to build new cyber-defences. As always, it’s not about [demonising a tool](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/38/dalla_necessita_alla_liberta.htm), everything depends on who uses it, on the goals it’s employed for, and, above all, on the ongoing struggle for hegemony in the field of AI.
